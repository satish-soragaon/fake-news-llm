About the Project

What is Fake News Detection?

Fake news detection is the application of advanced Natural Language Processing (NLP) to mathematically analyze text and determine its authenticity. In an era of rampant digital misinformation, automated classification systems help filter out fabricated narratives, clickbait, and misleading articles before they spread.

How the Model Works

The core intelligence of this system is powered by a multi-stage machine learning pipeline carefully trained on over 5,000 verified articles:

  • Explicit Preprocessing: Incoming text is strictly cleaned (punctuation removed, lowercased, and filtered for NLTK English stopwords) to isolate meaningful vocabulary.
  • TF-IDF Vectorization: The cleaned text is converted into a mathematical matrix using Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency, weighing the importance of specific words.
  • Classification: The vector is evaluated by a Passive Aggressive Classifier, which was selected after rigorous comparative benchmarking against Logistic Regression, Naive Bayes, and Random Forest algorithms.
